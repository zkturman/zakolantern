The Great Rollens Disappearance

Back in 1979, Camp Rollens was your typical mid-western American summer camp. Like every other year, the counselors came together to celebrate after all the kids had finally returned home. The morning after the party in 1979, however, no one left camp. No counselor arrived home. It was one of the greatest mass disappearances in history, but with no witness or bodies, police eventually abandoned the search. It became known as The Great Rollens Disappearance.

The police had only ever found one lead. Near the camp, a cult had established itself earlier in the year. They called themselves the 'Threads of Doom'. No one knew much about them aside from their frightening appearance. Notes in police reports mention worshipping bones and the belief in a prophet named 'Doomweaver'. Their religious texts featured unholy rituals and omens. The cult proved to be a dead end, however. All of the members had disappeared the same night as the counselors.
